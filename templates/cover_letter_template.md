# Cover Letter Template

Tone: real, clear, warm, and specific. Avoid generic AI phrasing. Do not repeat the resume; use details that make your motivation believable.

## Paragraph 1: Who I Am + Role + Availability

Dear [Hiring Manager/Team],

I am applying for the [exact job title copied from JD] at [Company]. I am currently focused on roles where I can combine business analysis, operations thinking, and English-Chinese communication in an international environment. I am available from [availability date] and would be excited to contribute to [one sentence about the role's core purpose].

## Paragraph 2: Why This Industry and Company

What to include:

- Company product, business model, market, or recent initiative.
- Explain the industry operating reality the company is managing, such as logistics-network reliability, institutional-client trust, cross-border growth, or supply-chain resilience.
- Why Germany matters if the company is international.
- Any event, network conversation, internship exposure, industry contact, or company research.
- Annual report, website, product page, or press release detail.

Draft:

What attracts me to [Company] is [specific business/product/team reason]. In [industry], [one sentence explaining the real business or customer challenge]. [Company]'s [product/model/market position] makes this role meaningful because [specific reason].

## Paragraph 3: Transferable Value - Why Me

Do not simply repeat resume bullets. Use a story or detail.

Possible themes:

- Cross-cultural empathy and English-Chinese communication.
- Rebuilding life in a new country and staying self-driven.
- Switching between roles, contexts, and identities while staying grounded.
- Clear communication, boundaries, and ownership.
- Innovation and interdisciplinary thinking.
- Business + brand strategy, tech/company growth, operations, or market expansion.

Draft:

Across my work and education, I have learned to stay calm in unfamiliar systems and turn ambiguity into next steps. In [experience/project], I [specific detail], which built [transferable capability]. In this role, that capability would transfer to [named daily responsibility from the JD] by [concrete mechanism]. I do not see operations, customer work, and strategy as separate boxes; I see them as connected ways to understand people, processes, and business outcomes.

## Paragraph 4: Interview Ask

I would welcome the chance to discuss how my background in [2-3 relevant areas] could support [Company/team]. Thank you for your time and consideration, and I look forward to the possibility of speaking with you.

Best regards,

[Name]

## Track-Specific Angles

Tier 1: Business Operations / Program Coordinator / Operations Analyst / Supply Chain / S&OP

- Emphasize execution, planning, coordination, process improvement, data, stakeholder clarity.

Tier 2: Customer Success / Solutions Consultant / Implementation Consultant

- Emphasize client empathy, structured problem solving, onboarding, product understanding, communication.

Tier 3: Category / Buying / Merchandise Planning / Fashion Business

- Emphasize market sense, consumer insight, brand/business strategy, analytical merchandising, cross-cultural taste and judgment.

## Anti-AI Checklist

- Include one concrete company detail.
- Include one personal detail that would be hard for another applicant to copy.
- Use shorter sentences when the paragraph feels too polished.
- Avoid phrases like "I am uniquely positioned", "dynamic environment", "leveraging synergies", or "passionate about excellence".
- Keep it under one page.
